Top 5 Endangered Species

Vaquita (Phocoena sinus): The vaquita is a small porpoise native to the northern part of the Gulf of California, Mexico. With an estimated population of less
than 10 individuals, it is considered the world's most endangered marine mammal. The primary threat to vaquitas is entanglement in illegal gillnets used for fishing, leading to accidental drownings.

Hainan Gibbon (Nomascus hainanus): Native to China's Hainan Island, the Hainan gibbon is one of the world's rarest primates. Conservation efforts have increased their numbers from around 15 individuals in the 1970s to approximately 30 today. However, they remain critically endangered due to habitat loss and limited genetic diversity.

Javan Rhinoceros (Rhinoceros sondaicus): Found primarily in Ujung Kulon National Park in Indonesia, the Javan rhinoceros is estimated to have a population of around 75 individuals. They face threats from habitat loss, poaching, and natural disasters like tsunamis and volcanic eruptions.

Amur Leopard (Panthera pardus orientalis): Native to the temperate forests of the Russian Far East and northeastern China, the Amur leopard is one of the world's most endangered big cats, with an estimated population of around 100 individuals. Habitat loss, poaching, and depletion of their prey base are significant threats to their survival.

Tapanuli Orangutan (Pongo tapanuliensis): Discovered in 2017, the Tapanuli orangutan is native to a small region in North Sumatra, Indonesia. With an estimated population of fewer than 800 individuals, it is the most recently identified and most endangered great ape species. Threats include habitat loss due to logging and hydroelectric projects, as well as hunting.
